# Superstore Sales Performance Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes multi-year retail sales data to identify revenue trends, regional performance differences, and product category profitability. The goal is to generate actionable insights that support data-driven business decisions.

## Business Questions
- How are sales trending over time?
- Which regions perform best and worst?
- Which product categories drive profitability?
- Where should leadership focus improvement efforts?

## Tools Used
- SQL
- Excel / Google Sheets
- PowerPoint (visualization & presentation)

## Key Insights
- Sales show consistent long-term growth with strong seasonality and recurring year-end spikes.
- The West region generates the highest total sales, while the South underperforms.
- Technology is the most profitable product category; Furniture generates the lowest profit.
- Some regions show high sales but low profitability, indicating optimization opportunities.

## Analysis Highlights
- Aggregated sales and profit by region, category, and time period using SQL.
- Cleaned and validated order-level data, removing duplicates while preserving negative profit values.
- Performed time-series analysis to identify trends and seasonal patterns.
- Created executive-ready visualizations to communicate insights clearly.

## Recommendations
- Align inventory and marketing spend with peak seasonal demand.
- Develop targeted strategies to improve performance in the South region.
- Prioritize high-margin Technology products.
- Review pricing and cost structures for Furniture to improve profitability.
